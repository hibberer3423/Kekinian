Why do millennials often change jobs?

Liputan6.com, Jakarta - More than a quarter of the world's population is millennial. It is estimated that in 2025, this generation born between 1980 and 2000 will fill three quarters of the workforce.


Many jobs currently adopt millennial values: modern, practical, flexible. Many startups or startups in Indonesia have their headquarters in big houses with comfortable facilities.


Does this make millennial loyal to the company? The answer is not necessarily no.

Quoted from Entrepreneurs, Sunday (02/10/2019), the millennial generation thinks, if they cannot be loyal to the company without three main things: compensation and salary, work-life balance or the balance of life and corporate culture.


It was based on a poll of 5,000 people who entered the millennial generation. The poll was held in October-November 2018 at the film recruitment company LaSalle, United States (US).

Colleagues also have an effect

Another study from the software company HubSpot, showed 56 percent of millennials and Gen-Z (the generation born in the mid-1990s to early 2000s) considered coworkers to be the most important thing at work.


The results of the poll also showed that the millennial generation wanted to work with an adequate salary including overtime pay and work flexibility including work from home.


Three out of four workers prefer the opportunity to work in a new place, with a new position and are not satisfied with their current career. Only 45 percent feel that a career is sufficient and intend to stay for a long time.


If the company still wants to employ millennials, the LaSalle report recommends that the company adopt a more employee-friendly culture.


Communication between employees relating to work must be two-way and seniors are encouraged to provide opportunities for juniors to work on a project.


Then, it is also important to make room for employees to add new abilities. Also, push with motivation to succeed and achieve goals.
